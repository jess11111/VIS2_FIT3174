Want to buy real estate in the heart of Melbourne?

The dataset provided by Tony Pino on Kaggle contain information about Melbourne housing market clearance from Jan 2016 to Aug 2018.

This visualisation zooms in on the council areas surronding Melbourne's CBD (see chart axis below).
We focus on 3 property types:

h = houses
t = townhouses
u = units
What type of property would most suit you? Which region offers you the best (or worst) buying solution based on your preferences? Lastly, based on recent data, during what times of the year should you make the purchase? Upon examination of this visualisation, answers to these questions may be made a little clearer.
The left-most chart shows some of the most and least sought after and populated council areas around metropolitan Melbourne. If you're on a budget, an unit in Bayside or Yarra City Council may be most suitable. Buying a house will cost you around twice as much. Despite the lower demand (or supply), houses in Maroondah appears to be the most expensive when compared to other councils.

The heatmap below indicates that 3-4 bedroom houses were most frequently purchased/listed. If you're after a 5+ bedroom property, a house may be your only option.

Once you've considered property type... WHERE exactly should you buy?

← Left map shows the building age distribution of properties sold. Newer properteis will have better amenities and lower maintenance overheads. The north-western suburbs such as Gowanbrae appears to have sold many newly developed properties. Conversely, the oldest buildings are concentarted in inner city suburbs such as Fitzroy, Middle Park and Kingsville. Older properties often have greater value in land compared to the actual building. As we can see, they are located in regions with well-established infrastructure, which means these properties possess factors that are beneficial future capital growth.

Right map shows the price relative to number of bedrooms . Suburbs that are just outside the CBD, such as Toorak and St Kilda, appear as the most expensive. You can use the dropdown tool to find these suburbs. An additional ~20 km away from these suburbs, a room becomes 2-3 times cheaper. →

.

← Left map shows the spatial distribution of properties that are less than 20-years-old (as of 2021). Using Selection tools, we can observe that in recent years, townhouse demand (or supply) have diminished considerably. In contrast, many more units have been sold in recent years as compared to ~20 years ago (particularly in Hawthorn and Glen Iris).

Right chart shows that you will receive less land for each dollar paid when the property is situated closer to the CBD (a square meter of land becomes more expensive). Particularly for houses, distance is slightly more influential on price than land size - meaning that when considering a property that either has large land or closer proximity to the city centre, you may find the former option to be more economical. Occasionally, you will find units in the city that sell for a comparably more valuable price (see small red dots near bottom). →

If you've considered location... WHEN is a good time?

On average, sales prices tend to be at the beginnging of the year and gradually increase throughout the months. For example, units were lowest at $560k in February (left) and at $1.6k/meter square of building in January (right). There evern appears to be an inverse relationship between units and townhouses - unit prices increase as townhouse prices decrease.
Although houses seem like the most costly type of property to purchase, townhouses are in fact more expensive per dollar value.
